Class Subjects

Zulu

Zulu is a Southern Bantu language of the Nguni branch spoken by more than 10 million people mainly in the Zululand area of the Kwazulu/Natal province of South Africa.

Swahili

Swahili is an official language of Tanzania and Kenya. It is also widely spoken in Uganda, the Democratic Republic of Congo and the Comoros Islands.

Croatian

Croatian is the official language of the Republic of Croatia. It is also an official language in Bosnia and Herzegovina, as well as regions of Austria, Italy, and Serbia.

Tahitian

Tahitian is a Polynesian language spoken mainly on the Society Islands in French Polynesia. Approximately 125,000 people throughout the world speak this language.

Finnish

Finnish is spoken by about 5 million people, most of whom reside in Finland. There are also notable Finnish-speaking minorities in Sweden, Norway, Russia, Estonia, Brazil, Canada, and the US.

Romanian

Romanian is spoken by approximately 25 million people as a native language, primarily in Romania and Moldova. Another 4 million people worldwide speak Romanian as a second language.
